Guide to writing a research paper:

"If we knew what it was we were doing, it would not be called research, would it?" Albert Einstein

Identify a Research Gap: Review recent AI literature to pinpoint areas lacking exploration or improvements that could be made to existing methods. (No idea is silly as long as it is specific enough)
Define a Clear Research Question: Formulate a precise, measurable, and impactful question or hypothesis to guide your study. (Research question is the key)
Conduct a Comprehensive Literature Review: Analyze existing studies to understand the state-of-the-art techniques and position your work within the field. (No pain, no gain, plus when we share, we have more 😄)
Select a Methodology: Decide on the algorithms, datasets, or frameworks that align with your research goals and ensure they are implementable within your resources. (One of our topics)
Experiment and Validate: Implement your solution, run experiments, and validate results using rigorous metrics and benchmarks. (Data and performance metric!)
Analyze and Interpret Results: Draw insights from the outcomes, comparing them with existing methods to highlight strengths and limitations. (Empirical)
Write the Paper: Structure the paper into standard sections (Abstract, Introduction, Methodology, Results, Discussion, Conclusion) while adhering to the target journal or conference guidelines. (Presenting is the other half of the work)
Seek Feedback: Share the draft with peers or mentors to identify areas for improvement and address potential weaknesses. (Us!)
Revise and Edit: Incorporate feedback, refine the language, and ensure the paper is clear, concise, and free of errors.
Submit to a Journal or Conference: Choose an appropriate venue based on scope, impact factor, and audience, and follow their submission process carefully. (YES!)
